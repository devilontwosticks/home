THE LOW DOWN ON SINGLE-CELL GENOMICS

In 2013 Nature Methods named single-cell genomics their "Method of the Year", within an atmosphere of claims that cellular resolution would enable functional genomics' equivalent of the human genome project. As co-steer of the Oxford Single Cell Biology Consortium, and current Head of Genomics in the recently founded Novo Nordisk Research Centre Oxford, I've helped drive both the technology and computational developments. In a talk aimed at functional genomicists, computational biologists, and liver/diabetes researchers, I'll provide an overview of the space and the realities being faced as part of my goal to integrate massively parallel gene editing and single-cell methods to model liver metabolism and identify type 2 diabetes drug targets.
